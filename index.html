Liberation in Context

The evolution of the fight for equal rights over time

Liberation in its purest form is setting one free from their boundaries or shackles. While individual liberation, such as leaving a toxic relationship or quitting a job with a 72-hour work week, is hard, it does not compare to the degree of difficulty of social liberation. Social liberation consists of efforts to enact lasting change in society, combat systeming discrimination, and tear down barriers between race, gender, etc.

Social liberation has great importance for our society because our actions today are foundational for future generations. However, there are major challenges in fighting for social liberation, including institutional inertia and oppression. We will explore the successes and failures of previous social liberation movements. In addition, we will also examine reasons for their successes, failures, and lasting impacts to help contextualize the Black Lives Matter Movement.

Liberation Today

Black Lives Matter Six Demands

End of Violence

End the war on black people.

Invest-Divest

Divestment from the institutions that criminalize, cage and harm black people; and investment in the education, health and safety of black people.

Reparations

Reparations for past and continuing harms.

Community Control

Community control of the laws, institutions and policies that most impact us.

Economic Justice

Economic justice for all and a reconstruction of the economy to ensure our communities have collective ownership, not merely access.

Political Power

Independent black political power and black self-determination in all areas of society.

The Black Lives Matter Movement

The Black Lives Matter Movement is a social liberation movement fighting against police brutality and racial profiling of African Americans. Similar to feminist movements, BLM is a continuation in a long history of the fight for equality, and furthers efforts of previous civil right leaders and movements. Additionally, while some of BLM’s objectives may have been seen as “radical” at first, like the Combahee River Collective, there have been strong efforts to help make their goals more accessible to society at large. This is in part due to the role of social media, which allows individuals in this organization to share their perspectives. Social media is a powerful tool which helps to humanize the movement, and make the stories of others more widespread and available. The Black Lives Matter Movement is an ongoing part of the history of social liberation movements, shows the importance of technology on modern day activism, and is an example of how social change is an ongoing process towards a better tomorrow.
